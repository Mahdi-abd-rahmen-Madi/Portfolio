Setting up a non-custodial wallet, A beginner's guide

Abstract :

Setting up a non-custodial wallet is crucial for securely managing digital assets as well as interacting with Decentralized Finance apps.

A non-custodial wallet gives its users complete control over their private keys without relying on third parties. However, the process of choosing the right type of wallet, securing and backing-up private keys, making transactions.. may seem daunting and off-putting for beginners.

In this beginner's guide, we'll walk you through the basics of setting up a non-custodial wallet.

Introduction :

Cryptocurrencies are becoming more and more popular these days, and many people are looking for ways to get involved in the exciting world of decentralized finance (DeFi). One of the first steps you'll need to take is setting up a non-custodial wallet, which is a digital wallet that allows you to store, send, and receive cryptocurrencies without relying on a third-party service.

If you're new to cryptocurrencies, setting up a non-custodial wallet might seem daunting at first. However, the process is actually quite straightforward;

1. Choose Your Wallet

For starters, you should select a wallet that fits your requirements. Although there are various kinds of wallets available, we suggest a software wallet for newcomers, a software wallet is an application or a browser extension that you can download and install on your computer or mobile device.

Some popular software wallets include:

  • AirGap wallet : A highly secure wallet, typically used as a cold storage for large amount of cryptocurrencies where the private keys are stored on a device that is not connected to the internet
  • Metamask wallet : a simple interface to interact with decentralized applications (dApps).
  • Exodus: A beginner-friendly wallet with a clean interface and support for multiple cryptocurrencies.
  • Trust Wallet: A mobile wallet with support for many cryptocurrencies.

Or make your own choice, to do that, consider using WalletScrutiny.

WalletScrutiny.com is a website that provides information on the security and privacy of various cryptocurrency wallets. It offers a database of wallet apps and rates them based on several factors, including open-source code availability, encryption, backup options…

2. Download and Install Your Wallet

Once you've chosen your wallet, the next step is to install it on your device through the download links provided on the wallet's official website.

Make sure to download your wallet from a trustworthy source, as scammers have been known to create fake wallets that resemble the genuine ones.

Links to the official websites mentioned above.

3. Set Up Your Wallet

After installation, the next step is to set it up. Although the exact steps may vary based on the specific wallet, here are some general guidelines to keep in mind:

  • Create a strong password using a random password generator such as KeepassXC.
  • Backup your seed phrase via a password manager, KeepassXC.
    Check out: Hardening non-custodial wallets.

4. Fund Your Wallet

After setting up your wallet, it's time to fund it. You can do this by transferring cryptocurrencies from a centralized exchange (CEX) such as Binanceor from another wallet via peer-to-peer (P2P) transactions.

To receive cryptocurrencies, you'll need to provide the sender with your wallet's public address. Your public address is a long string of characters that looks something like this: 0x62aDc3F46De3c6dAE4a08c905a9e83f8a2027Fb4.

To send cryptocurrencies, you'll need to enter the recipient's public address and the amount you want to send.

5. Use Your Wallet

Well done! You have successfully set up your non-custodial wallet. You can now start using it to send and receive cryptocurrencies, monitor your balances, and dive into the exciting world of DeFi.

Just remember to always keep your seed phrase and password in a safe place and to use best practices for keeping your digital assets secure.

Conclusion :

To sum up, creating a non-custodial wallet is a crucial requirement for those interested in cryptocurrency. Although the process may appear intimidating initially, it is simple and easy to follow. With a little care and patience, you can set up your wallet in no time.
