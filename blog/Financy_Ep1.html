2. The Case of Inflation:

Inflation is a measure of how much the purchasing power of a currency decreases. It is the percentage rate at which the prices of goods and services are rising.

Inflation rates are calculated by comparing the cost of a set of goods or services over time. The inflation rate is usually expressed as an annualized percentage, such as 2% per year. means that a unit of currency effectively buys less than it did in prior periods.

What changed ?

The inflation rates have been on the rise in recent years, due to a variety of factors including increased demand for goods and services, higher oil prices, and quantitative easing.

According to Trading Economics, it has reached an all-time high of 8.6% during last month (August 2022)

3. What can you do to protect yourself from inflation:

By investing in assets that will hold their value, investors can not only protect themselves from inflation but also generates a positive cash flow that will help them maintain their lifestyle without depending on a paycheck from their day jobs.

Cryptocurrency has been around for more than a decade, but it is only recently that it has become a viable investment option. Cryptocurrency can be used to buy and sell goods or services and offers the potential to replace fiat currency in the future. The key component of cryptocurrency is that it is decentralized.

This means that no single entity controls the cryptocurrency and every transaction occurs on a peer-to-peer basis. It also means that the price of a digital currency can fluctuate significantly and unpredictably, as well as not being tied to any one country's economy.

Rather then sticking to the old methods of using saving accounts, crytocurrency tackle the issue with a modern approach : “Stacking”

What is stacking ?

According to coinbase, stacking is a way of earning rewards for holding certain cryptocurrencies.

How does Stacking work ?

Simply put, if you own a cryptocurrency that allows stacking, you can stake some of your holdings and earn a percentage-rate reward over time.

This process usually happens via a “stacking pool” which you can think of as being similar to an interest-bearing savings account.

While stacked, your crypto holdings becomes part of the process: Cryptocurrencies that allows stacking use a “Consensus mechanism” called proof of stake,

which ensures that all transactions are verified and secured without a middle man (usually a bank or a credit card company)

What is Proof of Stake ?

A newer consensus mechanism with the implication of increasing speed, efficiency and lower transction fees, In which transaction are validated by people who are already invested in the blockchain via stacking, their stacked tokens act as a guarantee of the legitimacy of any new transaction they add to the blockchain.

Notice : The network chooses validators based on the size and the duration of their stacking.

What are the advantages of stacking ?

Long term crypto holders looks at stacking as a way of making their assets work for them passively by generating rewards and reinvesting their rewards in the same coins leading to a compound return on investment(ROI).
